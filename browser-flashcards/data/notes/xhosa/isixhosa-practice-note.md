# ### isiXhosa Noun Classes

> ##### |Personal Nouns|
> personify
> 
> **(um - aba/abe/ab)** *ngeli a-wa*
> 
> 1. umuntu - abantu (person/people)
> 2. umhlobo - abahlobo (friend/friends)
> 3. umlungu - abelungu (white person/white people)
> 
> -phula
> umphuli (person who breaks things)
> 


> ##### |Personal proper nouns / kinship / some personal nouns|
> 
> **(u - oo)**
> 
> 1. ubhuti - oobhuti (brother/brothers)
> 2. utata - ootata (father/fathers)
> 3. uJohn - ooJohn (John/more than one Johns)



> ##### |Non-personal, anatomical terms, names of trees, some nouns derived from verbs, miscellaneous|
> manner of/ adverbify
> 
> **(um - imi)** *ngeli u-i*
> 
> 1. umvubu - imivubu (hippo/hippos)
> 2. umthi - imithi (tree/trees)
> 3. umpu -imipu (gun/guns)
> 
> -phula
> umphulo (manner of breaking)
> 

> ##### |Anatomical terms(in pairs), individuals of ethnic group, personal nouns, borrowed words, some animal names|
> 
> **(ili/i - ama/am)** *ngeli li-ya*
> 
> 1. idolo -  amadolo (knee/knees)
> 2. isele (frog)
> 3. ixesha - ixesha (time)
> 4. amanzi - amanzi (water)
> 5. amandla - amandla (strength) *ngeli ya-ya*


> ##### |all languages, ordinal no.s, words from other langs beginning with s|
> expert
> 
> **(isi/is - izi)**
> 
> 1. isiXhosa
> 2. isikolo (school)
> 3. isibini (2nd)
> 4. isidenge - izidenge (fool/fools)
> 5. isifo (disease)
> 
> -phula
> isiphuli (expert of breaking things)
> 


> ##### |Most animal names, few personal nouns, certain nouns derived from verbs|
> act of
> 
> **(i - ii/izi)**
> 
> 1. ingwe - izingwe (leopard/leopards)
> 2. intombi - iintombi (girl/girls)
> 3. inthetho (speech)
> 4. indlela (way)

> ##### |Certain nouns derived from nouns|
> act of/state of - make something abstract 
> 
> **(ulu/u)**
> 
> 1. uthando (love)
> 2. uluthi - izinthi (stick/sticks)
> 
> -phula
> uphulo (act of breaking)
> 

> ##### |Certain abstract nouns|
> make something abstract
> 
> **(ubu/ub)**
> 
> 1. ubuntu (humanity)
> 2. ububele (kindness)
> 3. ububi (ugliness)
> 4. ubuhle (beauty)
> 5. ubusi (honey)
> 6. ubusuku (night)
> 
> -phula (break)
> ubuphuli (state of breaking)
> 

> ##### |Gerunds|
> 
> **(uku/uk)**
> 
> 1. ukufa (to die)
> 2. ukutya (to eat)
> 3. ukhweza (to do/ doing)
